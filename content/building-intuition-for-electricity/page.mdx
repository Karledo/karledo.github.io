## Electrical Potential 

Electrical potential at a point is the work done per unit charge to move a charged particle from infinity to that point.

Electrical potential is simply just electrical potential energy per unit charge.

But what is electrical potential energy?

Electrical potential energy is the work that would need to be done to, move a single positive charge from infinity. The greater the size of the charge, the greater its potential energy at any given point in the electric field.

The change in the electrical potential of a charged particle when it moves from one point in the electrical field to another point, is called the *potential difference*, between the two points.

Now, you may hear people mention the word, voltage, but in most cases, what they're actually referring to potential difference. 

When you hear the word potential, you might think of the phrase, potential energy, and you should. But you have to be careful, because a volt, is not a unit of energy and a lot of sources are *very* pedantic about this distinction.

To put it simply, potential difference is to electrical potential energy the same way height is to gravitational potential energy. 

Think about rolling a ball up a hill. The greater the height the ball is rolled up the hill, the greater its gravitational potential energy. Once we release the ball from that height, it rolls down the hill and its gravitational potential energy is converted into kinetic energy.

You can think about potential difference in the same way. Different points of the electric field have different *heights*, and by *dropping* to different positions on the field, we can convert the electrical potential energy to more useful forms of energy, like light, or thermal energy.

## Series circuits

In a series circuit, the total potential difference is divided across the resistors in proportion to their resistances. This means, resistors with a larger resistance get a larger share of the potential difference dropped across.
